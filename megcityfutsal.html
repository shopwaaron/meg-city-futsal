What is futsal?

A faster,
sharper game.

Futsal is a small-sided indoor game played with a smaller, low-bounce ball. Instead of big fields and long balls, players are constantly on the ball in tight spaces — making quick decisions, shielding defenders, and combining with teammates.

It's the game that produced many of the world's best players, and it builds skill and confidence at a remarkable pace.

  • More touches on the ball than any outdoor session
  • Faster decision-making and better field awareness
  • Sharper technique — dribbling, turning, passing, shooting
  • Better fitness through quick, repeated bursts of play
  • Direct boost to outdoor soccer performance
  • For all levels — no prior futsal experience needed
Who it's for

Ages 7 to 12.
Boys & girls.

We welcome players from Rancho Cucamonga and communities throughout the Inland Empire. Some of our newest players have never played organized soccer; others are competitive club players adding futsal to their training. Both fit right in.

Players are grouped by age, experience, and skill level. All new players are evaluated during their first session — coaches need to be present for this, so new families should contact us directly to schedule their first session and ensure the right staff is available.

What a first session looks like

60–90 minutes.
Three phases.

1

Warm-up & skill work

Coaches guide players through dribbling, turning, passing, receiving, and shooting drills built around real-game situations.

2

Small-sided games

3v3 and 4v4 games that give every player tons of touches and chances to apply what they're learning.

3

Quick coaching points

Teaching moments between rounds — so players keep growing without losing the fun.

Between seasons

The Off-Season.

Our two main seasons run Jan–May and Aug–Nov. December, June, and July fall in between — training doesn't fully stop, but it looks a little different.

What off-season training looks like

Development-focused,
not mandatory.

Sessions are still held regularly during the off-season, but the focus shifts from competitive preparation to individual player development — technique, touches, and fundamentals in a lower-pressure setting.

December, June, and July are also peak vacation and family time, so we don't expect full attendance during these months. Off-season training is there for players who want the extra reps, not a requirement.

A few things to know

Why it's a bit
less predictable.

✓
Sessions continue

Regular development-focused training generally continues through the off-season.

✓
Attendance is flexible

Family trips and vacation time are expected — no penalty for missing off-season sessions.

+
Coaches may take breaks

Sessions may pause for a stretch during the off-season so coaches can rest too.

+
Dates aren't fixed

This is why our next season's start date isn't set in stone until it's close — off-season timing shifts year to year.

Questions about off-season training?

Reach out directly for the current off-season schedule and status.

Our mission

Building complete
players in SoCal.

Our mission is to provide youth soccer players in Southern California with an exciting and challenging futsal program that opens doors to compete in regional, national, and international competition. Our goal is to give young players the proper training and exposure to increase their opportunities in both futsal and soccer.

Meg City Futsal is a year-round futsal training program built to help players reach their full potential and experience the highest levels of competition. We make every effort to develop a better overall soccer player through futsal. Players who consistently attend and show development may be invited to represent the club in competitive settings.

Our values

Three things
we hold to.

Respect

For coaches, referees, parents, teammates, and opponents. Always and without exception.

Kindness

The youth futsal and soccer community is competitive, but we are all connected and work together.

Fun

Experience is key. We hope every player enjoys the game and stays in it for life.

The team behind the team

Meet our staff.

Our coaches bring decades of futsal and soccer experience from across the globe — all focused on developing the next generation of complete players.

Director

Joel Perales

Joel holds a USSF "C" license and has 10+ years of futsal experience. He worked for one of the top MLS academies — New York Red Bulls — where he was first introduced to futsal. He has traveled the world to compete and study the game, including time in Spain, Brazil, Portugal, and the UK, and credits his development to two great mentors: Rodrigo Nunes (Rio de Janeiro Federation) and Jordi Palau (Catalunya / FCB).

Head Coach

Gyan Chudasama

Gyan recently returned to Meg City Futsal after living in Barcelona for over a year. During that time he immersed himself in European football culture, continuing his coaching education and volunteering with multiple professional clubs — Fundació Esportiva Grama, La Florida C.F., and Footvia International Academia. That international experience now informs everything he brings to the court.

Head Coach

Anthony Soriano

Anthony is a cornerstone of the Meg City community — a coach and a parent who sees the game from both sides. With 20+ years of experience coaching at the recreational, club, and high school levels, he brings valuable perspective, genuine passion, and a work ethic that sets the tone for every session. He holds a USSF "D" License and APFC Positional Play Certificate.

Admin Assistant

Lisa Fisher

Lisa is the organizational backbone of Meg City Futsal. With 25+ years of experience working with students, players, and families across soccer and futsal, she brings the planning, communication, and execution skills that keep the club running smoothly behind the scenes.
